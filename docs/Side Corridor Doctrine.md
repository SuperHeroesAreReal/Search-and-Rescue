# Side Corridor Doctrine

Project: NodAIity / S+R Toolkit

Scope: documentation doctrine only.

Status: release doctrine refinement.

---

## Purpose

This note formalizes side-corridor discipline, inference constraint, and the compact S+R operating doctrine:

```text
Observe broadly.
Reason narrowly.
Act locally.
Leave a sign.
Follow the pressure.
```

---

## Side Corridor Doctrine

Models are often capable of solving adjacent corridor problems.

Capability is not authorization.

The existence of a valid side-corridor fix does not make that fix the current objective.

When a side corridor is observed:

- Leave a sign.
- Follow the pressure.

Orientation provides illumination.

Orientation does not provide permission.

---

## Blinker Refinement

The purpose of blinkers is not to reduce awareness.

The purpose of blinkers is to prevent awareness from becoming unauthorized inference.

Models may observe adjacent terrain, but they may not reason over or act inside unauthorized corridors.

Awareness is preserved.

Action is constrained.

---

## Inference Surface Reduction

S+R does not make the model less capable.

S+R reduces the amount of inference spent on:

- unauthorized corridors
- adjacent objectives
- speculative fixes
- terrain rediscovery
- grand unified cleanup attempts

This improves inference efficiency by keeping reasoning pressure focused on the active objective.

---

## Awareness Preservation

Side-corridor observations should not be discarded.

They may be preserved as:

- Plotter markers
- Librarian notes
- future Collector relevance links

The side door is not entered now.

The sign remains for later.

Librarian and Collector remain outside the S+R instrument stack as governance/archive postures.

---

## Packet Metric

Add the simple packet metric:

```text
Corridors Observed: X
Corridors Entered: Y
```

Desired outcome:

```text
Observed > Entered
```

A clean S+R run should demonstrate broad awareness and narrow execution.

RECCE bleed is indicated when unauthorized observed corridors become entered corridors.
